## Problem

A musician selling merchandise, a Substack writer selling a course, and an artist selling prints each route payments through 2–4 intermediary platforms that take 10–30% combined, add delays before creators can access funds, and give the creator no way to automatically share revenue with collaborators, producers, or the fans who helped grow their audience.

## Solution

Direct storefronts where revenue splits among collaborators, co-creators, and communities execute automatically at the moment of purchase — with instant settlement, programmable royalty flows, and no platform intermediary taking a cut of every transaction.

## Why Ethereum

Enforcement: revenue sharing terms execute automatically at the moment of sale — collaborators receive their share instantly without waiting for a platform to calculate and remit, and no party can unilaterally change the split.
