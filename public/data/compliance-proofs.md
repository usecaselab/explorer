## Problem

Proving regulatory compliance today requires disclosing sensitive business or personal data to auditors and regulators, creating privacy risks. Inspection and enforcement records are also scattered across agency databases with no tamper-evident chain of custody.

## Solution

Zero-knowledge mechanisms (such as ZK-KYC and selective disclosure) that let regulated entities prove compliance without revealing underlying sensitive data, backed by tamper-evident audit logs.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: zero-knowledge proofs let entities demonstrate compliance cryptographically — proving the fact without exposing the underlying data, which is impossible with traditional disclosure frameworks.
