## Problem

Social platforms rely on centralized moderation teams and opaque algorithms to decide what is spam, manipulation, or harmful — systems that don't scale, lack accountability, and are trivially captured by political pressure. Reputation-weighted community moderation and verified-human signals exist as concepts but have no neutral infrastructure to run on that any platform could adopt.

## Solution

Modular moderation infrastructure combining community-written notes on contested content, reputation-weighted flagging where established accounts carry more signal, and stake-based curation where moderators put tokens at risk on their judgments — all running on a shared layer that any platform can read without building its own moderation stack from scratch.

## Why Ethereum

Composability: reputation scores, stake records, and community note histories live on a shared layer — any platform can plug into the same moderation signal without rebuilding the infrastructure, and no single company controls what gets suppressed.
