## Problem

When a food safety recall is issued, tracing contaminated product back to the source takes days or weeks because lot IDs change at every handoff between grower, packer, distributor, and retailer — each maintaining their own disconnected records — meaning contaminated product stays on shelves while investigators manually reconstruct the chain.

## Solution

A shared lot ID standard that persists immutably through every handoff in the supply chain — so when a retailer scans a product, they can trace it back to the specific farm field and harvest date in seconds, and a recall can be executed against a precise subset of product rather than pulling entire categories from shelves.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: each handoff in the supply chain is recorded as an immutable event — making recalls faster to execute and food fraud detectable by anyone with access to the chain.
