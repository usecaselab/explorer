## Problem

Scientific experiments that can't be replicated are often later traced to undisclosed variations in reagents, cell lines, or materials — a batch of antibodies that performed differently from what was published, a cell line that had been unknowingly contaminated — but there's no standard way to record or share the provenance of physical research materials that would let other labs understand what they're actually working with.

## Solution

Immutable provenance records for biological materials, reagents, and research tools — tracking origin, handling history, quality control results, and cross-references to experiments that used each lot — so that replication failures can be traced to their source.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: provenance records are cryptographically attested at each step of a material's lifecycle — a replication lab can independently verify they're working with the same reagent lot and handling conditions as the original study, rather than trusting self-reported descriptions.
