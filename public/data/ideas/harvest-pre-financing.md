## Problem

A subsistence farmer who needs $300 to buy seeds and fertilizer at planting time has no collateral for a bank loan and no trading history with formal lenders — so they borrow from local moneylenders at 60–100% annual interest rates, or go without inputs and accept a lower yield, trapping them in a cycle where they can never accumulate enough surplus to escape the next season's dependency.

## Solution

Pre-harvest financing backed by tokenized future crop receipts — letting farmers collateralize their standing crop against verified planting data, weather feeds, and historical yield records, with repayment automatically deducted from the proceeds when the harvest is sold.

## Why Ethereum

Enforcement: repayment executes automatically from harvest sale proceeds when the crop is sold — lenders can extend credit against verifiable onchain collateral rather than paper promises, and farmers don't need to make a separate manual repayment.
