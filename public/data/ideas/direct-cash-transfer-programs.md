## Problem

Billions of people without bank accounts cannot receive aid efficiently because traditional transfer systems depend on banking infrastructure and charge high fees through multiple intermediaries. Administering recurring universal basic income payments at scale adds a second layer of overhead: heavy bureaucratic processes for identity verification, eligibility checks, and distribution make such programs expensive to run and slow to reach recipients.

## Solution

Digital wallet-based transfer infrastructure that reaches unbanked populations directly at minimal cost — supporting both targeted aid disbursements and recurring automated payments, using decentralized identity and smart contracts to handle eligibility verification without centralized administrative overhead.

## Why Ethereum

Composability: stablecoin transfers reach anyone with a mobile wallet — no bank account required, and the recipient can access the broader onchain financial ecosystem from day one.
