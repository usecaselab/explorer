## Problem

Private firearms sales in most US states have no mechanism for buyers to verify that a weapon wasn't stolen and no way for sellers to confirm a buyer isn't prohibited — a gap that exists partly because any centralized registry is politically untenable (the Tiahrt Amendment prohibits a searchable federal database) and partly because existing background check infrastructure (NICS) is unavailable for private sales in most states. The result is that stolen firearms circulate in secondary markets with no provenance trail.

## Solution

A voluntary, privacy-preserving provenance system where firearm transfers are recorded as encrypted attestations — a buyer can verify a weapon isn't flagged as stolen and a seller can confirm the buyer passed a background check, without either party's identity being stored in a searchable registry or accessible to government without a warrant.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: ZK proofs confirm provenance and background check status without creating a searchable ownership database — enabling voluntary compliance for private sales without triggering the political opposition that blocks centralized registry proposals.
