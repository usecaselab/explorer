## Problem

Intercompany transactions and multi-entity consolidation require each party to independently record and reconcile the same economic event — a process that consumes 30-40% of the time in monthly close cycles and makes audit preparation expensive because auditors must independently confirm balances with counterparties through a manual confirmation process that takes weeks.

## Solution

A shared ledger where both counterparties to a transaction confirm the same record at the point of origination — eliminating the reconciliation step during close and giving auditors real-time access to pre-confirmed balances rather than requiring them to send confirmation letters and wait for responses.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: every transaction is confirmed by both counterparties at origination on a shared, tamper-evident ledger — the monthly reconciliation step that drives close cycle length and audit cost is eliminated by construction.
