## Problem

Citizen participation in budgeting, voting, and policy feedback is limited to infrequent, low-trust mechanisms that provide no auditable proof that inputs were counted or acted upon. Formal elections compound the problem: existing e-voting systems ask voters to trust a proprietary black box counted their vote correctly, with no way for individual voters to verify their ballot was included without the operator confirming it.

## Solution

End-to-end verifiable voting using zero-knowledge proofs — letting each voter confirm their ballot was counted correctly while keeping votes secret — combined with participatory budgeting, petitions, and policy consultation platforms where every input is recorded and outcomes are publicly auditable.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: votes and budget inputs are cryptographically recorded — participants can independently verify that their contribution was counted and acted on, restoring trust in the process.
