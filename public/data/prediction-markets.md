## Problem

Governments, businesses, and researchers rely on polls, surveys, and expert panels to forecast elections, policy outcomes, and economic events — mechanisms with no accountability for being wrong and no way to aggregate the dispersed knowledge held by the people closest to the situation.

## Solution

Open markets where participants buy and sell positions on the outcome of real-world events, with payouts executing automatically when oracle-verified results are confirmed — turning forecasting into a mechanism that rewards accuracy and aggregates distributed knowledge.

## Why Ethereum

Enforcement: payouts execute automatically when oracle-verified outcomes are confirmed — no bookmaker needed to hold funds or settle positions, and no ability to manipulate results after the fact.
