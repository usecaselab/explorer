## Problem

FDA 21 CFR Part 11 requires electronic records for pharmaceutical and medical device manufacturing to be attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, and accurate (ALCOA) — but most manufacturers store batch records in mutable databases where a quality engineer can alter a result after the fact without detection, and reconciling records across suppliers for a single product lot requires weeks of manual document retrieval.

## Solution

Append-only batch records that capture every production step, quality test, and deviation with cryptographic attestation at the moment of recording — satisfying ALCOA requirements structurally rather than through policy, and enabling instant lot-level traceability across the supplier chain when a recall is triggered.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: production data is cryptographically committed at the time of recording, making post-hoc alteration detectable — regulators get an audit trail that satisfies ALCOA by construction rather than relying on SOPs that can be circumvented.
