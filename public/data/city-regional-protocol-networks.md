## Problem

Coalitions like C40 Cities and the Global Covenant of Mayors have enrolled hundreds of cities in public emissions reduction pledges — but compliance tracking relies on self-reported data submitted to central secretariats, with no neutral mechanism for member cities or the public to verify whether commitments are being met or to detect when cities quietly abandon targets.

## Solution

A shared ledger where city governments record verified emissions data, policy milestones, and adaptation measures — with attestations from IoT sensor networks, third-party auditors, and satellite monitoring — creating a publicly auditable record that any city, funder, or citizen can inspect.

## Why Ethereum

Verifiability: emissions records are cryptographically attested by independent data sources and immutably logged — member cities can't retroactively revise their historical commitments, and progress is verifiable by any party without trusting the coalition's secretariat.
