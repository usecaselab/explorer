## Problem

Academic credentials are issued as PDFs locked in institutional databases, skills acquired across employers and platforms exist in disconnected silos, and workers lose their ratings and reputation every time they switch platforms. NGO workers and journalists face the same problem in higher-stakes form: they must repeatedly prove affiliations in hostile environments while sharing full identity documents puts them at risk of surveillance and retaliation.

## Solution

Tamper-proof, portable credentials — diplomas, micro-credentials, work history, ratings, union membership, press credentials — that workers and professionals own and carry across platforms, employers, and jurisdictions, with selective disclosure so only the relevant claim is shared in each context.

## Why Ethereum

Composability: credentials issued by any institution on a shared layer are readable by any platform or verifier without bilateral agreements — a journalist's press credential, a nurse's license, and a freelancer's rating history all live in the same portable wallet.
