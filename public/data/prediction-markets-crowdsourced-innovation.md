## Problem

Research funding committees allocate billions based on grant applications reviewed by small expert panels, with no mechanism to aggregate what the broader scientific community actually believes about which hypotheses are likely to replicate, which drugs will fail trials, or which research directions will prove fruitful — leaving funding systematically biased toward incremental work from established labs.

## Solution

Open prediction markets where researchers stake tokens on the outcomes of specific scientific claims — will this trial replicate, will this drug hit its primary endpoint — with payouts flowing to those who were right. Market prices surface collective scientific belief in real time, and accumulated winnings serve as a credibility signal for funding allocation.

## Why Ethereum

Enforcement: payouts execute automatically when oracle-verified outcomes are confirmed — participants can't manipulate settlement, and the market's track record is publicly auditable, making it a credible input to funding decisions.
