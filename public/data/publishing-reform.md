## Problem

Academic recognition systems only reward first-author publications, leaving researchers who generate datasets, perform reviews, or replicate studies with no formal credit for work that is essential to science. Peer reviewers perform skilled labor for free on behalf of journals that charge institutions thousands per year — with reviews disappearing after use and no portable track record that follows the reviewer across journals.

## Solution

A market for peer review where journals post requests, qualified reviewers bid and are paid automatically on acceptance, and completed reviews become part of a portable verifiable track record — combined with credentials for dataset generation, replication work, and other non-publication contributions that carry the same weight as authorship.

## Why Ethereum

Composability: review contributions and payment history live on a shared layer readable by any institution or funding body — reviewers carry a verifiable track record across every journal they've worked with, not just those that maintain their own internal records.
